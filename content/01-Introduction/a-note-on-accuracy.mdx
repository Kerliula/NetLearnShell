# This course was built to understand, not to certify.

The person behind NetLearnShell is not a network engineer. This course grew out
of a personal study of computer networking — inspired by Richard Feynman's
observation that the best way to truly understand something is to teach it.

So that is what this is. A attempt to teach networking in order to understand it
better — written by someone working through the same material you are.

## What this means for you

The explanations here prioritize intuition over precision. In some places that
means a concept is simplified to make it click before the full complexity is
introduced. In others, it means an analogy is used where a formal definition
would be more accurate.

Occasionally it may mean a mistake slipped through.

## Treat it as a starting point

Nothing here should be taken as a authoritative reference. If something feels
off, trust that instinct — cross-check with the Kurose & Ross textbook, an RFC,
or another source. The Resources tab in the sidebar exists exactly for this.

The goal of this course is to get you to the point where you can read those
authoritative sources and actually understand them. That is the finish line.
