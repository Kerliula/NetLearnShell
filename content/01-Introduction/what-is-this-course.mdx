# NetLearnShell - A Guided Tour of Computer Networking

This is a **guided tour** through computer networking — built around the ideas in
_Computer Networks: A Top-Down Approach_ by Kurose & Ross, but written to feel
less like a textbook and more like a conversation.

## Along the way you will

- Explore how data travels across the globe in milliseconds
- Break down the protocols that make the internet work
- See the layers — from physical wires to the browser
- Build intuition through diagrams, animations, and interactive visuals

## Learn by Seeing, Not Just Reading

Where words fall short, diagrams take over. Where theory becomes abstract,
interactive visuals and animations help make invisible systems feel tangible.

## Learn by Experimenting

A real terminal command brings networking back to earth — you will run
`ping`, `traceroute`, `dig`, and other tools directly in your browser,
watching the internet respond in real time.
