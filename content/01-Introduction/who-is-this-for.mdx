# Who’s this connection for?

This course is for anyone who has ever wondered how the internet actually works
— not at a surface level, but properly, from the ground up.

## You will get the most out of this if you

- Write code, even occasionally — as a developer, student, or hobbyist
- Have used the internet your whole life but feel like it is a black box
- Want to understand what happens below the HTTP request your code makes
- Are preparing to study networking more formally and want a gentler on-ramp

## You do not need to be

- A systems programmer or network engineer
- Familiar with Linux or the command line (though it helps)
- A computer science graduate

If you can read a `for` loop and you know that websites involve a server
somewhere — you are ready.

## A word for the self-taught

Much of networking education is aimed at certification tracks — memorizing
commands, passing exams. This course takes the opposite approach. The goal is
intuition first, vocabulary second. If you have been self-teaching your way
through software, this course is written with you in mind.
